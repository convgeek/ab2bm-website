**Primary CTA button in nav:** All 10 sites have a primary action button. Common labels: "Contact Us," "Request Info," "Build Your Campaign," "Schedule a Consult," "Get Started."

```

```

Subnavigation (dropdowns) are common for Services and Resources. Solutions organized by objective (Generate/Engage/Accelerate) or by program type (Content Syndication, ABM, Intent, Display).

## Homepage Section Patterns

### Section Sequence (Most Common Order)

1. Hero with headline + primary CTA
2. Client logo strip ("Trusted by...")
3. Services/programs overview (3-column or tabbed)
4. Audience/reach stats OR proof section
5. Case study highlights or testimonials
6. Resources/blog preview (2-3 articles)
7. Footer CTA ("Ready to get started?")

### Hero Section

- **Headline pattern:** Action-oriented. Examples: "Attract More. Engage More. Convert More" (Activate), "Unmatched Lead Management and Data Governance" (Integrate), "Your Buyers Are Everywhere. Are You?" (NetLine), "Demand EXCELLENCE Delivered" (INFUSE)
- **Subheadline:** 1-2 sentences explaining who they serve and what outcome they deliver
- **Primary CTA button:** Always present. "Contact Us," "Request Info," "Build Your Campaign," "Talk to an Expert"
- **Hero visual:** Stock photography of business professionals or abstract data/tech imagery (not audience photography)
- No site uses video hero backgrounds prominently

### Client Logo Strip

Present on 9/10 sites. Usually appears immediately below hero or as a second section. Enterprise logos (Dell, Salesforce, ServiceNow, Google, Adobe, AWS, Microsoft, etc.) displayed in a row or carousel. Typically 8-15 logos. Labeled "Trusted by industry leaders" or similar.

### Services / Programs Section

All sites present programs in a structured grid or tabbed layout. Common approaches:
- 3-column cards (most common)
- Funnel-stage framing: Identify / Engage / Qualify (Activate), Generate / Engage / Accelerate (DemandWorks)
- Numbered/named program list with brief description + "Learn More" link
- Programs shown: Content Syndication, ABM, Webinars/Webcasts, Intent Data, Display/Programmatic, Nurture Programs, BANT Qualification, Telequalification

### Audience / Reach Stats Section

Present on 7/10 sites. Common stat types:
- Total addressable audience size (e.g., "212.5M+ B2B profiles" — INFUSE; "125M monthly visitors" — NetLine)
- Geographic reach (global region breakdowns)
- Industry verticals covered
- Seniority/decision-maker breakdown (e.g., "29% C-suite")
- Number of brands/properties in network (e.g., "300+ websites" — Headley)

### Case Studies / Testimonials Section

All 10 sites include social proof. Two modes:
- Testimonial quotes (attributed, with name/title/company) — 8/10 sites
- Metrics-led case study callouts (e.g., "6.6x pipeline boost — Trend Micro") — 7/10 sites
- Full case study pages (separate, linked) — 8/10 sites

### Resources / Blog Preview

Present on 8/10 sites. Usually 2-3 cards previewing recent blog posts, reports, or webinars. Serves double duty: SEO and buyer education.

### Footer CTA

8/10 sites close with a pre-footer CTA section before the footer links. Common pattern: short value statement + single "Contact Us" or "Let's Talk" button.

## Feature Landscape

### Table Stakes (Users Expect These)

Features/pages that appear on 4+ sites. Missing these makes Ab2bm look incomplete or untrustworthy.

| Feature | Why Expected | Complexity | Notes |
|---------|--------------|------------|-------|
| Hero section with clear headline + CTA | First impression; buyers decide in seconds | LOW | Needs to state WHO the audience is and WHAT programs exist |
| Client logo strip (enterprise logos) | Credibility shorthand; missing = "who are these people?" | LOW | Need 8-15 real client logos; rotating carousel acceptable |
| Named programs/services page | Buyers need to know what they're buying | MEDIUM | Should have Content Syndication, Webinars at minimum; BANT, ABM as extensions |
| Audience description page or section | Primary value prop for a media/lead gen vendor is the audience | MEDIUM | Must include: segments, job titles/functions, company size, geography, industry; Ab2bm has MSP/MSSP angle to emphasize |
| Case studies (3+ with metrics) | B2B buyers require proof before initiating contact | MEDIUM | Need client name OR anonymized persona, challenge, solution, quantified result |
| Client testimonials (attributed) | Qualitative counterpart to case study metrics | LOW | 4-6 quotes minimum; name, title, company |
| Resources / Blog | Indicates the vendor is alive and thinking; helps SEO | MEDIUM | Need at least 6 posts at launch; ongoing cadence |
| Contact page with form | The conversion point — missing = no leads | LOW | Form, email, phone at minimum; no multi-step required for v1 |
| About Us page | Trust and context for a vendor a buyer has never heard of | LOW | Team photo/bios optional; company story and focus required |
| Footer with nav links | Navigation fallback; standard web expectation | LOW | Solutions, Company, Resources, Contact, legal links |
| Privacy Policy + GDPR/CCPA statement | Legal requirement; buyers check for compliance | LOW | Required; CCPA opt-out expected |
| Primary CTA button in nav | Navigation to conversion | LOW | "Contact Us" or "Request Info" is the industry standard label |

### Differentiators (Competitive Advantage Opportunities)

Features that appear on 1-3 sites or are done poorly across the board.

| Feature | Value Proposition | Complexity | Notes |
|---------|-------------------|------------|-------|
| Dedicated Audience page with specific stats | Most sites describe audiences vaguely; specific numbers (audience size, % decision-makers, industry breakdown) signal data depth | MEDIUM | Ab2bm should lead with MSP/MSSP specificity — no competitor does this; "50,000+ managed service providers" style stat would be unique |
| Methodology / How It Works page | Reduces "black box" anxiety; builds trust before contact | LOW | Headley's 7-step process is a strong model; buyers want to know how leads are sourced, validated, delivered |
| Team page with real photos | Humanizes a category full of faceless vendors | LOW | Leadium/INFUSE use team photos effectively; Activate feels personal; most feel corporate |
| Conversational Geek content integration | Ab2bm's unique asset — original full-funnel content for IT pros enriches the audience value | HIGH | No competitor has a content production partner embedded in the brand; this is a real differentiator if surfaced well |
| Program comparison table | Most sites make you guess how programs differ | LOW | "Single Touch vs. Double Touch vs. Nurture" side-by-side would reduce sales friction |
| Audience segment deep-dives | Going deeper than a stat page — describing the PERSONAS (IT Director at a 250-person MSP) | MEDIUM | No competitor does persona cards for their audience; creates empathy with the buyer |
| FAQ page | Only 1 competitor has this; B2B buyers have common questions | LOW | Questions like "What is your lead validation process?" and "Do you offer guaranteed CPLs?" reduce sales cycle friction |
| Intent data / in-market signal callout | Most competitors mention intent data but don't explain it; clear plain-English explanation stands out | LOW | Even a short section explaining "We know your audience is actively researching because..." differentiates |
| Client success metrics in nav (case study sub-items) | Activate does this — case study titles with metrics visible in nav dropdown | LOW | Unusual but creates trust before a buyer even clicks |

### Anti-Features (Present on Competitor Sites, Hurt Credibility or UX)

| Feature | Why Requested | Why Problematic | Alternative |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|-------------|
| Vague hero headlines ("A modern approach to the B2B buying journey") | Sounds strategic | Says nothing; buyers don't know what you sell | Specific: "Content syndication and webinar programs that reach 50,000+ IT professionals" |
| Generic stock photography (handshakes, laptops, blur) | Looks "professional" | Indistinguishable from any vendor; contributes to "same as everyone else" perception | Real team photos, audience-representative imagery, data visualizations |
| Hiding pricing entirely with no signal | Keeps options open | Frustrates buyers; competitors with any signal (Leadium shows ranges) win the consideration set | At minimum, explain the engagement model (CPL-based, program minimums, custom quotes) |
| Overlong homepage with 15+ sections | Thoroughness | INFUSE homepage has 13+ distinct sections; buyers abandon; content further down rarely seen | 6-7 focused sections; let inner pages carry the depth |
| Services presented as jargon without explanation | Assumes domain knowledge | "SoftBANT" or "Multi-Touch Leads" mean nothing to a new buyer | Lead with the outcome ("Reach IT decision-makers at the moment they're researching"), then introduce program names |
| No audience stats at all | Avoids commitment | Looks like the vendor doesn't know their own database; Activate is the clearest example of this gap | Any quantified audience claim is better than none |
| Client login buried or broken | Operational necessity | Creates a dead-end navigation item for non-clients; distracts new visitors | Only add client portal when operationally ready; use separate subdomain |
| Wall-of-logos without client names or context | Appears impressive | Anonymous logos are less credible than named clients with industries | Named clients, or logos with quote attribution |

Compliance badges (SOC2, GDPR, CCPA) in footer: present on Integrate and INFUSE; not standard at smaller vendor scale but worth including if applicable.

## Feature Dependencies

```
Audience Page
    └──requires──> Audience data/stats (real numbers from Ab2bm)

Case Studies
    └──requires──> Client permission OR anonymized stories
    └──requires──> Quantified metrics

Resources / Blog
    └──requires──> CG content production (6+ posts at launch)

Programs / Services pages
    └──requires──> Program naming decisions (what Ab2bm actually sells)

Contact / Inquiry flow
    └──requires──> Form backend (Supabase or form service)
    └──enhances──> CRM/email notification

Client Logo Strip
    └──requires──> Permission from clients to use logos
```

### Dependency Notes

- **Audience Page requires real data:** The page only works with real numbers. This means Ab2bm needs to provide audience stats (database size, MSP/MSSP count, job function breakdown) before the page can be built. This is a blocking dependency on content, not code.
- **Case Studies require minimum 3 stories:** A page with 1-2 case studies reads as cherry-picked. 3+ gives pattern credibility. If real client case studies aren't available at launch, anonymized stories ("A Fortune 500 cybersecurity vendor...") are an acceptable bridge used by several competitors.
- **CG content pipeline affects Resources launch readiness:** The blog/resources section is hollow without at least 6 posts. CG's content production should target this before site launch.

## MVP Definition

### Launch With (v1)

Minimum viable site for a buyer who finds Ab2bm organically or is referred by a sales rep:

- [ ] Homepage with hero, logo strip, programs overview, audience stats, testimonials, blog preview, footer CTA
- [ ] Services / Programs page covering Content Syndication and Webinars at minimum
- [ ] Audience page with segments, stats, and MSP/MSSP callout
- [ ] About page with company story and CG partnership mentioned
- [ ] Contact page with form + direct contact info
- [ ] 3 case studies (real or anonymized)
- [ ] 6+ blog posts (CG-produced)
- [ ] Footer with all standard links
- [ ] Privacy Policy and CCPA opt-out

### Add After Validation (v1.x)

- [ ] Methodology / How It Works page — when sales team identifies "how do you source leads?" as a common objection
- [ ] Expanded program pages (ABM, BANT, Intent Data) — when those programs are being actively sold
- [ ] FAQ page — when common pre-sales questions are documented
- [ ] Program comparison table — when buyers request side-by-side
- [ ] Team / Meet the Team section — when team wants to be public-facing

### Future Consideration (v2+)

- [ ] Client login / portal — only when there's operational infrastructure to support it
- [ ] ROI calculator — high complexity, needs real data to be credible
- [ ] Regional content variants — only if international programs are active
- [ ] Newsletter / email capture — when content production cadence is established
- [ ] Podcast section — if CG or Ab2bm launches a podcast
